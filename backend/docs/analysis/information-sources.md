## Overview

The financial information ecosystem can be understood as a spreading chain, where information flows from fundamental data sources through institutional reports to human-generated content. Each stage in this chain has different characteristics in terms of importance, accessibility, complexity, reliability, popularity among public viewers, and information density.

---

## Analysis Framework

### Metrics Explained

1. **Importance**: The critical value and impact of the information for financial decision-making
   - Scale: High / Medium / Low
   
2. **Easy Access**: How readily available and accessible the information is
   - Scale: Easy / Moderate / Difficult
   
3. **Complexity**: How difficult it is to understand and use the information effectively
   - Scale: Low / Medium / High
   - Note: Lower complexity means easier to understand and apply
   
4. **Reliability**: The trustworthiness and accuracy of the information source
   - Scale: High / Medium / Low

5. **Popularity**: How popular the information source is among public viewers (demand/desire to access)
   - Scale: High / Medium / Low
   - Note: Measures how much the general public actively seeks out this information

6. **Information Density**: The amount of valuable information per unit of content (e.g., HBR has high density with comprehensive analysis, while local media may have lower density with more filler content)
   - Scale: High / Medium / Low
   - Note: High density means more actionable insights and data per article/report; low density means more conversational, repetitive, or surface-level content

## Detailed Breakdown

### 1. Fundamental Information

#### Price of Copper / Rare Materials
- **Importance**: High - Critical raw material prices directly affect manufacturing costs and supply chains
- **Easy Access**: Easy (copper) / Moderate (rare materials) - Public commodity exchanges provide real-time data
- **Complexity**: High - Requires understanding of supply-demand dynamics, global markets, and logical induction to interpret impact
- **Reliability**: High - Data comes from regulated commodity exchanges
- **Popularity**: Low - Generally only sought by professionals, traders, and industry analysts; not commonly accessed by general public
- **Information Density**: High - Raw data points are highly concentrated; each price point represents significant market information

#### Government Law / Politics
- **Importance**: High - Regulatory changes can dramatically impact markets and industries
- **Easy Access**: Easy (Laws) / Hard to collect (Politics) - Government websites provide official laws; political information is scattered across multiple sources and requires aggregation
- **Complexity**: Medium - Requires legal and policy interpretation skills
- **Reliability**: High (Laws) / Medium (Politics) - Official laws are authoritative; political analysis varies
- **Popularity**: Low (Laws) / High (Politics) - Government laws are rarely accessed by general public unless directly affected; politics generates high public interest and engagement
- **Information Density**: High (Laws) / Medium (Politics) - Legal documents are dense with specific regulations and requirements; political content varies but often includes more commentary and less actionable information

#### Employee Rate / Interest Rate
- **Importance**: High - Key economic indicators that drive market sentiment
- **Easy Access**: Easy - Published regularly by central banks and statistical agencies
- **Complexity**: Low (Interest Rate) / Medium (Employee Rate) - Interest rates are straightforward; employment data needs context
- **Reliability**: High - Official government statistics
- **Popularity**: Medium (Employee Rate) / High (Interest Rate) - Interest rates are widely discussed and sought after by general public (affects loans, savings); employment data is moderately popular
- **Information Density**: High - Economic indicators provide concentrated, actionable data points with significant market implications

#### Weather
- **Importance**: Medium - Affects specific sectors (agriculture, energy, retail) but not universally critical
- **Easy Access**: Easy - Weather services provide free access
- **Complexity**: Low - Direct and easy to understand
- **Reliability**: High - Scientific weather forecasting
- **Popularity**: Medium - Regularly checked by general public for daily planning, but not specifically for financial purposes
- **Information Density**: Low - Weather data is straightforward but limited in financial context; requires sector-specific interpretation to extract value

#### Company Financial Reports (財報)
- **Importance**: High - Essential for fundamental analysis and investment decisions
- **Easy Access**: Moderate - Requires access to company filings (SEC, company websites)
- **Complexity**: High - Requires financial analysis expertise to interpret
- **Reliability**: High - Regulated financial reporting
- **Popularity**: Medium - Actively sought by investors and traders, but not by general public who typically rely on summaries or analysis from other sources
- **Information Density**: High - Financial reports contain extensive quantitative data, detailed financial statements, and comprehensive business information in structured format

### 2. Institution Reports

#### Professional Institutions (FED, HBR, Bloomberg, Reuters)
- **Importance**: High (FED, HBR, Reuters) / need pay (Bloomberg) - Authoritative sources with deep analysis; Bloomberg requires subscription for full access
- **Easy Access**: Easy (FED, Reuters) / need pay (HBR, Bloomberg) - FED and Reuters are publicly available; HBR and Bloomberg require subscriptions
- **Complexity**: Low to Medium - Professional reports include detailed explanations and context
- **Reliability**: High - Established institutions with editorial standards and fact-checking
- **Popularity**: Medium (FED, Bloomberg, Reuters) / Low (HBR) - Financial news from Bloomberg and Reuters is moderately popular; FED reports are followed by investors; HBR is more niche, targeting business professionals and academics
- **Information Density**: High - Professional institutions provide comprehensive, well-researched analysis with extensive data, case studies, and actionable insights; HBR is particularly known for high-density content with deep analysis

#### Normal Media (Fox, SET, Local Media)
- **Importance**: Medium to High - SET provides official market data (High); general media varies (Medium)
- **Easy Access**: Easy - Freely accessible through various platforms
- **Complexity**: Low - Written for general audience
- **Reliability**: Medium to High - SET is authoritative (High); general media varies by outlet (Medium); local media often lower (Low)
- **Popularity**: High (Fox News) / Medium (SET) / Medium (Local Media) - Mainstream media like Fox News has high viewership; SET is popular among local investors; local media varies by region
- **Information Density**: Medium (Fox News, SET) / Low (Local Media) - Mainstream financial media provides moderate density with news and analysis; SET offers high-density market data; local media often has lower density with more filler content and less in-depth analysis

### 3. Human Reports

#### KOL Information Sharing (股癌, Podcasters, YouTubers)
- **Importance**: Medium - Can provide insights and market sentiment, but not primary data
- **Easy Access**: Easy - Freely available on platforms
- **Complexity**: Low - Designed for entertainment and education, easy to consume
- **Reliability**: Medium - Varies by individual; may include opinions, biases, or incomplete analysis
- **Popularity**: High - Extremely popular among general public; KOLs like 股癌 have large followings; podcasts and YouTube financial content attract millions of viewers seeking accessible financial information
- **Information Density**: Medium (股癌) / Low (Podcasters, YouTubers) - KOLs like 股癌 provide more concentrated insights and analysis; podcasts and YouTube videos often have lower density due to conversational format, repetition, and entertainment-focused content

#### Normal People Sharing (Thread, PTT, Reddit)
- **Importance**: Low - Primarily sentiment and anecdotal information
- **Easy Access**: Easy - Social media platforms are freely accessible
- **Complexity**: Low - Informal, conversational format
- **Reliability**: Low - Unverified, may contain misinformation, rumors, or personal opinions
- **Popularity**: High - Very popular among general public; platforms like Reddit, PTT, and Thread have millions of active users discussing stocks, investments, and market trends; easy to access and engage with
- **Information Density**: Low - Social media posts are typically conversational, repetitive, and contain significant noise relative to actionable information; requires filtering to extract valuable insights

## Recommendations

### For Information Aggregation Systems

1. **Prioritize High-Importance, High-Reliability Sources**
   - Focus on fundamental data and professional institution reports
   - These provide the most value with highest trustworthiness

2. **Consider Complexity in User Interface Design**
   - High-complexity sources (raw commodity prices, financial reports) need interpretation tools
   - Low-complexity sources (media reports, KOL content) can be presented directly

3. **Accessibility Strategy**
   - Easy-access sources can be integrated directly
   - Moderate/difficult access sources may require API partnerships or subscriptions

4. **Reliability Weighting**
   - Use reliability scores to weight information in decision-making algorithms
   - High-reliability sources should have more influence than low-reliability sources

5. **Information Density Consideration**
   - High-density sources (HBR, Bloomberg, financial reports) provide more value per unit of content
   - Low-density sources (social media, local media) may require filtering and aggregation to extract value
   - Balance density with accessibility and user preferences

6. **Information Chain Analysis**
   - Track how information flows from fundamental sources through institutions to human reports
   - Identify information lag and distortion at each stage
   - Note how information density decreases as information spreads through the chain
